# The Crystal King Project

Anton Beauvilliers is a good Martian citizen.  He has no official status at any level of Martian government.  He has no special ties or allegiance to any particular corporation. The crime syndicates have no idea who he is.  In all respects, he is a quietly successful solo entrepreneur making a small fortune selling outback adventure safaris to off-world elite.  

Many of these safaris are networking opportunities bringing together firearm enthusiasts and those who make them.  Anton is, by anyone's definition, a private arms dealer.  He is the sort of person that nations, corporations, and militaries publicly denounce, privately hire, and secretly fund.

Lou, in his search for a set of no-questions-asked power armor, found his way into Anton's underground circle.  Initially, Anton flat out said no, "I can't broker a deal to equip a private citizen in power armor.  My contacts just won't do that." Over the last few months, Lou has been slowly pressuring Anton on this request, building enough of a relationship to bridge that gap.  Hopefully.

Anton's finally caved.  Finding a source for military-grade power armor for a private citizen, off-books, is still too tall an order.  But Lou's persistence has convinced Anton that he represents a powerful force, with deep pockets, and a just cause.  What that cause might be Anton absolutely does **not** want to know.

Anton has been sitting on a story that came to him one evening in the lodge, reminiscing about old times with a pair of triple-centenarians who happened to be old recon marines back in the days before the Swap, when Mars was Mars, the *real* Mars, not this woke multi-world GovCentral bullshit.

They told Anton all about a deeply secret research facility where they were assigned as power armor operators for a new type of armor that uses an active internal nano-forge for on-the-fly configuration, repair, and even protection.  To these old vets, it was an ancient secret that doesn't even matter anymore.  Three hundred year old armor tech is essentially useless by today's standards.  Even our own government has forgotten all about it.  

But Anton has a lot more experience with modern tech, especially off-world tech.  He wondered to himself, *of course ancient nanotech, running ancient software, will build ancient armor tech... Useless?  Software can be undated.  Will ancient nanotech running modern software build... modern armor?"

Anton had a problem with this revelation, however.  Unbound, unregulated, nanotech is still massively taboo.  Outright illegal in every place he could think of.  Who could he sell this too without getting landing himself in a deep, dark federal prison for the rest of his days.

What he needs is someone working outside the major governments, someone with deep pockets, someone with the power to create a new life for him, totally untraceable.  Someone off-world, using the armor for off-world activities.  Someone willing to take on the risk of *finding* the site and getting it back in operation on their own dime, assuming all the risk.

Lou has entered the chat.

For 100 million, a brand new ego ID, a new biosynth that will last him his next 200 years, sparing him of any need for ever resleeving again, Anton will tell you what he's learned of this site.

## Icaria Badlands
Way down south, at the very bottom corner of the Martian Outback, at the very edge of what most people consider habitable climate, sits the Icaria Badlands.  This subpolar region is dotted with a handful of tiny townships of just a few thousand people (and often less than that).  These towns are highly isolated, and the people usually live there for that very reason.

What does the subpolar border of the Martian Outback look like way down at 52 degrees south latitude?

![SouthOutback52Lat.jpg](/img/user/_meta/_attachments/SouthOutback52Lat.jpg)

A few hundred kilometers east of Hussey Basin is a long thin scar on the land known as Parker Canyon. Overlooking a section of this canyon is a small rise of red sandstone cresting about 600 meters above the badlands it sits in.  This is Yarly Peak.  

#### Leeward
In the eastern wind shadow of Yarly peak, the small town of Leeward was founded, sometime about 460 years ago.  Little has changed in Leeward since this time.  Leeward Township is home to about 4200 people and isn't really known for anything in particular.  It's a place for people that wanted an empty horizon, open land, and refuge from the dust storms back in the days when dust storms were a common hazard.  

Leeward is an incorporated holding of the regional Martian government.  The mayor of Leeward is essentially in the chain of command leading all the way up to GovCentral Parliament, and the Executive.

#### Fort Berenson
On the western side Yarly Peak is the old military base that was built shortly after Leeward was founded, against quite a bit of local protest.  

The first section of the base, Fort Berenson, was originally built as a polar communication hub and early warning base.  At that time, the Space Elevators were in heavy use, and the equatorial orbits were highly regulated.  It was believed at the time that any covert landings by off-world entities would likely come in from the poles to avoid the equatorial monitors.  With Mars' north polar ocean a natural barrier, the southern polar region was considered the main weak link.  This doctrine was abandoned long ago.

The nearby canyon proved to be an excellent training arena for Marine infantry and powered cavalry units.  The Parker Field Training Annex was added to serve that need.  Once Mars realized most military conflicts would be fought in low- or zero-g, most training facilities were moved into orbit or offworld.  Parker Field was closed shortly after. 

During the base's prime, Macky Air Field was added to allow heavy lift aircraft to transport mobile armor units and the supplies and equipment needed to service such.

Macky Air Field is still in service as a private air field, though the long heavy lift runway is no longer in service.

Fort Berenson is technically still a reserve forces base, but public records show fewer than 20 permanent billets there.  

#### Wayne Township
With the base in full operation, the small town of Wayne was constructed to house and service the non-military dependents of base personnel.  

Since the shutdown of the bases, Wayne has become much more like Leeward in its culture and purpose.  Its current population is recorded as 3941.

Wayne is currently wholly owned by Ares (an Isotoxal subsidiary).

#### Brightly Township
Perched on the lower northern slope of Yarly Peak is the newest town of Brightly.  Brightly is a tourist trap that serves as a home base for outback safaris (competitors of Anton's), hiking in the canyon, and is home to a group of conservationists intent on reintroducing several species of raptor to the canyon ecosystem.  Eagles, vultures, falcons, and harriers mostly.

Brightly is a commonwealth chartered under the Central Plains University of Agriculture and Wildlife, which (believe it of not) gets 60% of its funding from the Royal Diamond Exchange.

#### Shifting
Shifting is a loose collection of tents, yurts, trucks, and mobile veterinary hospital that parks at the southern base of Yarly Peak when it isn't roving the badlands with its teams of conservationists, ranchers, cattlemen, and ecologists.

#### Ranches
The entire Icaria Basin is home to thousands of ranchers and homesteaders spread out across tens of thousands of square kilometers of space.  Most go weeks, even months, between visits to population centers, and many of these people consider a trip the Leeward's markets and supply shacks with its 4200 citizens as "going into the city".

# Crystal King Project

Anton's vets claim to have actually piloted the prototypes.  They recall taking them of a underground bunker/lab through a tunnel into Parker Field and down into Parker Canyon for trial runs.  They compared the state of the defunct training base against the way they remembered it when they were younger, when it was an active training base.  Seeing it shuttered and quiet saddened them, even as they lumbered through the old parade ground in state of the art nanotech power armor.

Exactly where that underground lab is, is up to to discover.  Getting it operational, that's on  you too.  Doing it while remaining undiscovered by a nearby town owned by the largest weapons manufacturer in the system?  Also on you.  

There is little to do regarding the comet in the near future.  Brandmauer teams are clearing other Barbaries.  Barbary Rock itself is being mondernized and prepped for use as a pirate base.  Skamander still has some work left but its deadline is far off.  You have the time to do this right.  You have the time to give Firewall a production line of not just power armor, but presumably a way to build small independent and completely unregulated nano-forges.  

This is your mission.
